Fermented Pickles

Description

Your taste buds will love these tangy, sour, crisp fermented pickles.

Fermenting dates back thousands of years! Using just water, salt, and the natural bacteria found on the skin of the cucumbers, this recipe is sure to be a hit!

Ingredients:

  • (3) cucumbers, sliced in spears
  • 10g (2 tsp) of kosher salt
  • (2) 1-pint (473ml) mason jars

Steps:

  1. Wash your cucumbers
  2. Slice off the ends of the cucumbers
  3. Slice cucumbers into spears. Make sure the spears are short enough to fit into jars with approx. 1/2 inch of head space.
  4. Add 10g (2 tsp) of kosher salt into bottom of each mason jar
  5. Pack spears tightly into jars
  6. Fill jars with water until spears are submerged
  7. If necessary, add a weight to keep spears submerged.
  8. Store jar in a cool, dark place
  9. Check your jars every couple of days, releasing presure from jars as necessary
  10. After about 14 days, you can start to taste your pickles. They are done once they reach your desired level of tangy/sour!
  11. Once you are satisfied, store in the refrigerator! Will keep for many months in the fridge
